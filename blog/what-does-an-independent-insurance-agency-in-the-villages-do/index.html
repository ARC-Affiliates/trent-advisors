How Does an Independent Agency Differ From a Captive Agent?

An independent agency differs from a captive agent in carrier access, contract structure, and client loyalty obligations.

Short answer: Independent agents represent the client across multiple carriers; captive agents represent one carrier to the client.

Independent vs captive: independent agencies are advantageous because they can shop 8-15 carriers and switch you to a better plan during the Annual Election Period without changing agencies. Captive agencies are a tradeoff because the agent earns commission from only one company — meaning even if a competitor offers a stronger 2026 Medicare Advantage plan in Sumter County, the captive agent cannot quote it. Who Regulates Insurance Agencies in The Villages, FL?

Insurance agencies in The Villages are regulated by the Florida Department of Financial Services and the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation.

Short answer: Florida DFS licenses individual agents; the Office of Insurance Regulation oversees the carriers whose products those agents sell.

Per Florida Statute 626.112, no person may sell insurance in Florida without a valid license from the DFS (source: leg.state.fl.us). Consumers can verify any agent's license at the DFS Licensee Search portal. Trent Advisors and other licensed independent agencies operating in The Villages must also comply with the federal CMS Medicare Communications and Marketing Guidelines (MCMG) when discussing Medicare products. Complaints can be filed through the DFS Insurance Consumer Helpline at 1-877-693-5236. For Medicare-specific concerns, residents near the Sharon L. Morse Performing Arts Center can also contact Florida SHINE (Serving Health Insurance Needs of Elders) for free counseling.

Insurance Myths vs Facts for Villages Residents

Myth: Using an independent agent costs more than going direct to a carrier.

Fact: Premiums are identical — federal and state rules prohibit agents from charging above the filed rate.

Myth: Medicare Advantage plans are always cheaper than Medigap.

Fact: Lower premiums often come with higher out-of-pocket maximums; total cost depends on health usage.

Myth: You can switch Medicare plans anytime.

Fact: Most changes are limited to AEP (Oct 15–Dec 7) or qualifying Special Enrollment Periods.

Myth: All agencies in The Villages sell the same plans.

Fact: Carrier appointments vary; not every agency can quote every plan in Sumter County.

#Red flags to watch for

  • Demands payment for a Medicare consultation (it should be free).
  • Cannot produce a Florida DFS license number on request.
  • Pressures same-day enrollment without comparing multiple carriers.
  • Discourages you from contacting Florida SHINE or 1-800-MEDICARE.
  • Makes unsolicited home visits without prior CMS Scope of Appointment.
  • Promises a "secret" or "exclusive" plan not available elsewhere.

In 2026, CMS continues enforcing the Third-Party Marketing Organization rules finalized under 42 CFR §422.2274 — meaning every Medicare sales call must be recorded and a Scope of Appointment form completed 48 hours before any meeting (source: ecfr.gov).
