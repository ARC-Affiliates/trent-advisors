What Are the Top Plan Categories Available in The Villages?

Plan categories are the legal classifications of coverage offered by insurers under federal and Florida law. For more information, see Estate Succession Planning The Villages FL: 2026 FAQ.

The seven main coverage types in The Villages are Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medigap, Part D, ACA marketplace, short-term medical, and dental/vision/hearing supplements.

Each does a different job. Medicare Advantage (Part C — private plans that bundle hospital, medical, and usually drug coverage) is popular in Sumter County because many plans include $0 premiums and dental. Medigap (a supplemental policy that pays Original Medicare's out-of-pocket costs) costs more monthly but gives nationwide flexibility — handy for snowbirds returning north of I-75.

  1. Original Medicare (Parts A & B) — federal baseline
  2. Medicare Advantage (Part C) — private bundled plans
  3. Medigap (Plans G, N, K) — pays Medicare gaps
  4. Part D — standalone prescription drug
  5. ACA Marketplace — for those under 65
  6. Short-term medical — gap coverage
  7. Dental, vision & hearing — ancillary policies

What Mistakes Cost Villages Retirees the Most Money?

Enrollment mistakes are decisions made outside protected windows that trigger penalties or lock-in periods.

The costliest errors are missing the Medigap 6-month open window, ignoring the Part D late penalty, and auto-renewing a plan whose formulary dropped your drug.

Myth: Once you pick a Medicare plan, you're stuck for life.

Fact: You can change Medicare Advantage and Part D plans every Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 – December 7).

Myth: $0 premium plans are always the cheapest overall.

Fact: Out-of-pocket maximums on $0 MA plans can exceed $8,850 per year — Medigap may cost less if you use a lot of care.

Myth: Any agent can sell any plan.

Fact: Agents must be specifically appointed and AHIP-certified for each carrier each year.

Myth: Switching to Medigap is easy any time.

Fact: After your initial 6-month window, Florida insurers can medically underwrite or deny you (per Florida Statute §627.6741).
