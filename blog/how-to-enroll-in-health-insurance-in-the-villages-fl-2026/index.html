What Are the 2026 Enrollment Periods in The Villages?

The 2026 enrollment periods are fixed federal windows: Medicare Annual Enrollment Period (AEP), Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (OEP), and the ACA Marketplace Open Enrollment Period.

Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare + Medigap

Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare with a Medigap policy: Medicare Advantage bundles Parts A, B, and often D into one plan with lower premiums but network restrictions. Original Medicare plus Medigap costs more monthly but allows any provider that accepts Medicare nationwide — useful for retirees who travel from The Villages to see family out of state.

A Typical Enrollment Scenario in The Villages

A common pattern in The Villages: a couple relocates from the Northeast to a home near Brownwood Paddock Square, both turning 66 within a year of arrival. They enrolled in Original Medicare during their Initial Enrollment Period but skipped Part D, assuming they would not need prescription coverage. After a new diagnosis requires a brand-name drug, they face a late-enrollment penalty of 1% per month for every month they delayed Part D — a permanent surcharge added to their monthly premium for life. The lesson Villages residents learn repeatedly: enroll in Part D or creditable drug coverage when first eligible, even if no prescriptions are needed at the time. The penalty compounds, and waivers are rare.

Who Should You Trust to Help?

Licensed help comes from Florida-licensed insurance agents, Medicare-certified brokers, and federally trained Navigators. As of 2026, only agents holding a current Florida Department of Financial Services license may legally sell health insurance in Sumter, Lake, or Marion counties.

Verify any agent's license at MyFloridaCFO.com before sharing personal information.

Credentials Legitimate Agents Should Have

  • Florida 2-15 license (Health and Life Including Variable Annuities) — verify at myfloridacfo.com
  • AHIP certification (America's Health Insurance Plans annual Medicare training)
  • Errors & Omissions insurance (minimum $1 million is standard)
  • Carrier appointments with multiple companies (single-carrier agents cannot truly compare)
  • Compliance with Florida Statute 626.611 governing agent conduct (source: leg.state.fl.us)

#Red flags to watch for

  • Pressure to enroll the same day without comparing options
  • Refusal to share their Florida license number
  • Door-to-door solicitation (prohibited for Medicare under CMS rules)
  • Promises of "free" benefits like groceries or cash cards without explaining the plan trade-offs
  • Requests for payment directly to the agent rather than the carrier
  • Vague answers about provider networks at UF Health or AdventHealth

#The Enrollment Process, Step by Step

  1. Step 1: Needs Review — List doctors, prescriptions, and budget. Identify whether Medicare, Marketplace, or employer coverage applies.
  2. Step 2: Plan Comparison — Compare at least three plans on premium, deductible, network, and formulary using Medicare.gov or HealthCare.gov.
  3. Step 3: Subsidy Check — For Marketplace plans, calculate Advanced Premium Tax Credit eligibility based on 2025 income.
  4. Step 4: Application Submission — Submit electronically with verified documents during the correct enrollment window.
  5. Step 5: Confirmation — Receive member ID, ID card, and welcome packet within 10–14 business days.
  6. Step 6: Annual Review — Re-evaluate every fall before the next AEP or Open Enrollment.
